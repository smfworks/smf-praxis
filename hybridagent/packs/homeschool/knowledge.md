# Homeschool reference knowledge

This is bundled grounding for the Homeschool pack. It is illustrative starter
content — replace it with your own state rules, curriculum, and family records.

## Recordkeeping basics
Many U.S. states ask homeschooling families to keep an attendance log, a
portfolio of representative work, and progress or grade reports. A common bar is
roughly 180 instructional days or about 900-1,000 hours per year; the exact
requirement varies by state, so verify locally before relying on a number.

## Notification and assessment
Some states require a yearly letter of intent and an annual assessment — either a
standardized test or a portfolio review by a certified evaluator. Keep copies of
intent letters and assessment results with the portfolio.

## Multi-grade planning
Teach siblings together with a shared "morning basket" (read-aloud, history,
science) and split only skill subjects (math, reading) by level. Loop scheduling
rotates subjects across days so no single subject is dropped when a day is short.

## Privacy
A child's name, work, grades, and assessments are private to the household. Do not
send them to anyone outside the family without the parent's explicit approval.
